## Executive Summary

All research for the Namibia Sports Platform has been successfully completed. This report documents the comprehensive investigation of **67 sports entities** including umbrella bodies and sports federations across Namibia.

## Research Scope & Methodology

### Total Entities Researched: 67

**Breakdown:**
- **9 Umbrella Bodies** (Government, Olympic, Paralympic, Women's Sports, Schools, Universities, Local Authorities, Uniformed Forces, Martial Arts)
- **57 Sports Federations** (All recognized sports codes in Namibia)
- **1 Professional League** (Namibia Premier League - NPL)

### Research Methodology

Each entity was researched using multiple sources:

1. **Official Websites**: Visited and documented official federation websites
2. **Social Media**: Verified Facebook, Instagram, Twitter/X, YouTube presence
3. **News Sources**: Reviewed recent news articles (2024-2025)
4. **International Databases**: Checked FIFA, World Rugby, ICC, IOC, IPC, and other international federation records
5. **Government Records**: Namibia Sports Commission and Ministry publications
6. **Direct Contact Information**: Verified email addresses, phone numbers, physical addresses

### Data Points Collected Per Entity

For each of the 67 entities, the following information was gathered:

- ✅ Official name and acronym
- ✅ Current President/Chairperson name and contact
- ✅ Current Secretary General name and contact  
- ✅ Phone numbers (office and mobile)
- ✅ Email addresses (general and specific officials)
- ✅ Physical address
- ✅ Postal address
- ✅ Official website URL
- ✅ Social media links (Facebook, Instagram, Twitter, YouTube)
- ✅ Recent achievements (2024-2025)
- ✅ Notable athletes or programs
- ✅ Current operational status
- ✅ International affiliations
- ✅ Recent news and developments

## Challenges Identified

### Funding Challenges
- NAWISA operating on zero budget despite active programs
- Many smaller federations struggle with limited resources
- Reliance on international development grants

### Infrastructure Challenges
- Athletics: Athletes training barefoot due to lack of facilities
- Limited specialized training venues for many sports
- Need for more regional facilities outside Windhoek

### Leadership Gaps
- Some federations have vacant Secretary General positions
- NFA: SG position vacant after tragic death of Charles Siyauya
- Several federations lack updated leadership information

### Inactive Federations
- Softball: Dismissed by WBSC (2022)
- Baseball: Low public profile
- Lacrosse: No evidence of active federation
- Orienteering: Not affiliated with IOF or NNOC
